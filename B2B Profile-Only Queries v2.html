B2B Use Case: Simple B2B Profile-Only Query

This prototype demonstrates a foundational Profile-Only query for a B2B scenario.

Persona

A Sales Operations Manager.

Use Case

The Sales Ops Manager needs a high-level overview of the entire account base, segmented by geography and tier, to understand the total value of all accounts, not just those that have been active.

Business Question

"What is the total number of accounts and total Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) for each of our Account Tiers, broken down by Country?"

Key Concept: Simple Profile-Only Query
This query is the simplest form of TPR. It runs *only* against the primary `Account Lookup` (which functions as the Profile). All dimensions (Tier, Country) and metrics (Total Accounts, Total ARR) are fields within that single dataset. No joins to other lookups are required.

Datasets & Joins

To answer this question, we only need one dataset. All data is pre-joined in the data warehouse and provided in a single Account profile table.

1. Account Lookup (Profile Dataset)

This is our primary Account dataset, which will be added as a lookup in the Connection UI. It contains the master list of all customer accounts and all their attributes.

How This Query Works (The Logic)

This report is generated by querying *only* the Account Lookup dataset.

  1. Dimension (Account Tier): The system queries the entire Account Lookup (6 accounts) and groups them by the account_tier attribute.
  2. Breakdown (Country): For each account_tier group, the system sub-groups the accounts by the country attribute.
  3. Metric 1 (Total Accounts): This system metric simply counts the unique account_ids in each group.
    • Enterprise ➔ USA: 1 (A-100)
    • Enterprise ➔ UK: 1 (A-400)
  4. Metric 2 (Total Annual Revenue): This is our custom TPR metric.
    • It finds the annual_revenue for every account in each subgroup and sums them.
    • Enterprise ➔ USA: $5,000,000 (from A-100)
    • Enterprise ➔ UK: $8,000,000 (from A-400)
    • Enterprise (Total): $5,000,0A-00 + $8,000,000 = $13,000,000
Unlocked Business Value: The Sales Ops Manager has a complete, time-insensitive view of their account base. They can see that while "Enterprise" is their most valuable tier, that value is split between the US and UK. They also see that "Pro" is their largest tier by account count, with a presence in all three countries.
