B2C Use Case: Profile-Minus-Event Query

This prototype demonstrates a Profile-Minus-Event query. This is the "dormant" or "inactive" use case, often called a "Right Anti Join".

Persona

A Loyalty Program Manager.

Use Case

The manager needs to quantify their "at-risk" customers. Specifically, they need to find all high-value "Gold" members who have not engaged with the brand recently. This list will be used for a targeted re-engagement email campaign.

Business Question

"Of our total 'Gold' level loyalty members, exactly how many *have not visited our website in the last 30 days*? I need a single number to track this 'Dormant Gold Member' segment."

Key Concept: Profile-Minus-Event
This query is achieved by creating a calculated metric that subtracts the *active* population from the *total* population.
(Total Gold Members) - (Active Gold Members) = (Dormant Gold Members)
We use the **`Total People`** (Profile-scoped) metric for the total and the standard **`People`** (Event-scoped) metric for the active count.

How This Query Works (The "MINUS" Logic)

This query works by breaking down by `Loyalty Tier` and then applying the subtraction row-by-row.

  1. For "Gold" Tier:
    • Total People (Total): The `Total People` (Profile-scoped) metric counts *all* people in the profile with `loyalty_tier = 'Gold'`. (Result: 3, counting p-101, p-102, and p-103)
    • People (Active): The `People` (Event-scoped) metric respects the "Last 30 Days" range and counts "Gold" members with an event. (Result: 2, counting p-101 and p-102)
    • Dormant People (Calc): 3 (Total) - 2 (Active) = 1
  2. For "Silver" Tier:
    • Total People (Total): Counts all profiles with `loyalty_tier = 'Silver'`. (Result: 2, counting p-201 and p-202)
    • People (Active): Counts "Silver" members with an event in the last 30 days. (Result: 1, counting p-201)
    • Dormant People (Calc): 2 (Total) - 1 (Active) = 1
